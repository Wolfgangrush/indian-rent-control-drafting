# State Exemplar — Delhi (Rent Control)

## Applicable State Rent Control Act
- **Act:** Delhi Rent Control Act 1958 (Act No. 59 of 1958)
- **Scope:** Premises in Delhi let for residence, business or any other purpose; certain categories exempt (Section 3 — premises with monthly rent exceeding ₹3,500 are outside the Act's protective umbrella for several grounds)

## Forum nomenclature
- **First instance:** `IN THE COURT OF THE ADDITIONAL RENT CONTROLLER, [PLACE]` / `IN THE COURT OF THE RENT CONTROLLER, [PLACE]`
- **Appellate:** Rent Control Tribunal — Section 38 DRC Act 1958 (Sr. Civil Judge designated as Tribunal)
- **Revisional:** High Court of Delhi — Section 39 DRC Act + Article 227 of the Constitution

## Eviction grounds (section-by-section)

| Section | Ground | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| 14(1)(a) | Non-payment of rent | Default in payment of rent within 2 months of notice of demand |
| 14(1)(b) | Sub-letting / parting with possession | Without obtaining landlord's written consent |
| 14(1)(c) | Misuser | Use for purpose other than that for which the premises were let |
| 14(1)(d) | Acquisition of alternate residential accommodation | Tenant has acquired vacant possession of, or has been allotted, another suitable residence |
| 14(1)(e) | Bona-fide personal need | Residential premises required bona fide by landlord for self / family; **Chapter IIIA summary procedure applies** |
| 14(1)(f) | Non-occupation for 6 months | Premises not occupied by tenant without reasonable cause for 6 months immediately preceding date of suit |
| 14(1)(g) | Substantial damage | Tenant has caused substantial damage to the premises |
| 14(1)(h) | Structural alteration | Tenant has, without written consent, made structural alterations |
| 14(1)(i) | Illegal / immoral purpose | Premises used for illegal or immoral purpose |
| 14(1)(j) | Nuisance / annoyance | Tenant or person residing with tenant guilty of nuisance / annoyance |
| 14(1)(k) | Change of user | Without written consent of landlord, contrary to terms of lease |

| 14C | Retired Central / Delhi Government employee | Special ground — Chapter IIIA summary procedure |

## Section 106 TPA notice position
- **NOT MANDATORY** for most grounds under the DRC Act 1958.
- Position in **V. Dhanapal Chettiar v. Yesodai Ammal, (1979) 4 SCC 214** directly applies — the DRC Act 1958 provides a self-contained code for eviction; Section 106 TPA notice is unnecessary for grounds under Section 14(1).
- However, where the landlord seeks possession on grounds outside Section 14 (e.g. determination of contractual tenancy for a non-statutory ground) or in a civil suit, Section 106 notice may still be required.

## Standard rent / fair rent fixation methodology
- **Sections 6-9** — Standard rent provisions
- **Section 6** — Standard rent defined; depends on date of construction / first letting (pre-1939 / 1939-1958 / post-1958 distinctions)
- **Section 9** — Application for fixation of standard rent to Rent Controller
- Cost-of-construction + reasonable return formula for post-1958 premises

## Rent deposit procedure
- **Section 27** — Tenant may deposit rent with the Rent Controller where landlord refuses to accept
- Deposit operates as valid tender; defeats Section 14(1)(a) default ground if made within prescribed time

## Summary procedure (if any)
- **Chapter IIIA (Sections 25A, 25B, 25C)** — SUMMARY PROCEDURE for petitions based SOLELY on Section 14(1)(e) (bona-fide personal need for residential premises) and Sections 14A / 14B / 14C / 14D
- Procedure: petition in prescribed form → summons issued in Section 25B(2) form → tenant must apply for leave to defend within 15 days of service → application supported by affidavit disclosing facts which would disentitle landlord from possession → if leave refused, eviction decreed forthwith
- Leave-to-defend jurisprudence: leading authorities include **Inderjeet Kaur v. Nirpal Singh, (2001) 1 SCC 706**; **Precision Steel & Engg. Works v. Prem Deva Niranjan Deva Tayal, (1982) 3 SCC 270**

## Pagdi / customary tenancy (if any)
- NOT statutorily recognized in Delhi
- Customary pagdi transactions in Old Delhi exist but draw no protection under DRC Act 1958

## Model Tenancy Act 2021 adoption status
- **NOT ADOPTED** in Delhi
- DRC Act 1958 continues to govern

## State-specific notes
- **₹3,500 monthly rent threshold** — Premises with monthly rent exceeding ₹3,500 are outside the protective umbrella of several Sections (notably Section 14) by virtue of Section 3(c) — for such premises, ordinary civil court jurisdiction under TPA + Section 106 notice apply
- **Chapter IIIA discipline is strict** — even one day's delay in leave-to-defend application is fatal; affidavit must disclose specific defence facts, not bare denials
- **Section 14(1)(e) requirement** — only the LANDLORD (not lessor by assignment within recent past) and only for RESIDENTIAL premises; commercial premises bona-fide need is NOT a ground under DRC Act 1958
- **Section 25B(8) revisional jurisdiction** — restricted revisional remedy from order on leave-to-defend / final eviction order under Chapter IIIA goes directly to High Court (no appeal to Rent Control Tribunal in Chapter IIIA matters)
- Rent Control Tribunal (Section 38) hears appeals from ordinary Section 14 orders; revision to High Court under Section 39
