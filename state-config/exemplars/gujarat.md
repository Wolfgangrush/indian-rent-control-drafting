# State Exemplar — Gujarat (Rent Control)

## Applicable State Rent Control Act
- **Act (current operative):** Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act 1947 (as adapted and amended for Gujarat post-1960 bifurcation)
- **Act (passed but NOT YET NOTIFIED):** Gujarat Rent Act 2011 — passed by Gujarat Legislative Assembly but NOT BROUGHT INTO FORCE due to constitutional challenge / awaiting notification; **verify current notification status** before drafting
- **Scope:** Premises in notified urban areas of Gujarat let for residence, business or any other purpose

## Forum nomenclature
- **First instance (Ahmedabad):** `IN THE COURT OF SMALL CAUSES AT AHMEDABAD`
- **First instance (other districts):** `IN THE COURT OF THE CIVIL JUDGE (SR. DN.) AT [PLACE]`
- **Appellate (Ahmedabad):** Appellate Bench of the Court of Small Causes, Ahmedabad
- **Appellate (other districts):** District Judge
- **Revisional:** High Court of Gujarat — Article 227 of the Constitution + statutory revisional provisions of the 1947 Act

## Section 106 TPA notice position
- **REQUIRED.** The 1947 Act framework operative in Gujarat retains the requirement of Section 106 TPA notice for determination of contractual tenancy.
- Gujarat High Court jurisprudence consistently requires Section 106 TPA notice along with statutory demand notice under Section 12 for default-of-rent ground.
- Practical discipline: serve composite notice under Section 12 / 13 read with Section 106 TPA.

## Standard rent / fair rent fixation methodology
- **Section 5(10)** — Standard rent defined; (a) rent at which premises were let on 1 September 1940, or (b) where premises were not let on that date — rent which Court determines as standard rent
- **Section 11** — Application for fixation of standard rent to the Court
- **Section 7** — Permitted increases (taxes, repairs, structural improvements)
- Cost-of-construction + reasonable return formula for premises constructed after the cut-off date

## Rent deposit procedure
- **Section 17** — Tenant may deposit rent in Court where landlord refuses to accept
- Deposit operates as valid tender; supports Section 12(3)(a) statutory defence to default-of-rent ground

## Summary procedure (if any)
- NO Chapter IIIA DRC-equivalent strict summary procedure under the 1947 Act
- Gujarat Rent Act 2011 (if/when notified) was designed to introduce shorter timelines and accelerated procedure — verify notification status

## Pagdi / customary tenancy (if any)
- NOT STATUTORILY RECOGNIZED in Gujarat
- However, **customary pagdi practice** exists in older neighbourhoods of Ahmedabad and Surat (and to a lesser extent Vadodara); pagdi transactions in these markets operate outside statutory framework and rely on contractual / customary enforcement

## Model Tenancy Act 2021 adoption status
- **NOT ADOPTED.** Gujarat has not notified MTA 2021 adoption
- Gujarat Rent Act 2011 (passed but unnotified) was a separate State initiative predating MTA 2021; status of either reform remains unresolved as of current verification

## State-specific notes
- **Continuing applicability of the 1947 Bombay Act** — post-1960 bifurcation of Bombay State into Maharashtra and Gujarat, Gujarat continued the parent 1947 Act with its own amendments; Maharashtra subsequently enacted the Maharashtra Rent Control Act 1999 (replacing the 1947 Act for Maharashtra), but Gujarat has not yet completed the equivalent transition
- **Gujarat Rent Act 2011 status** — passed by Gujarat Legislative Assembly; challenged on several grounds; not brought into force; awaiting notification or further legislative action. Always verify current status before drafting under either framework
- **Section 12(3)(a) statutory defence** — Gujarat retains the Bombay-Act framework where a tenant who pays/deposits arrears within prescribed time after notice is protected from eviction on default ground; strategically significant defence
- **Appellate Bench of the Court of Small Causes Ahmedabad** is a specialised appellate forum for Ahmedabad rent matters (analogous to Mumbai's Appellate Bench)
- **Comparative hardship test** under Section 13(2) applies for bona-fide requirement ground
- Premises let to certain categories (banks, corporates, statutory bodies, etc.) may attract exemptions — verify exemption schedule
