# State Exemplar — Maharashtra (Rent Control)

## Applicable State Rent Control Act
- **Act:** Maharashtra Rent Control Act 1999 (Act No. XVIII of 2000)
- **Scope:** Applicable to premises let for residence, education, business, trade, storage, etc., in areas to which the Act extends (notified Greater Mumbai, Pune, Nagpur and other urban areas)
- **Replaces:** Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act 1947 (in Maharashtra)

## Forum nomenclature
- **First instance (Mumbai):** `IN THE COURT OF SMALL CAUSES AT MUMBAI`
- **First instance (other districts where District Judge retains jurisdiction):** `IN THE COURT OF THE DISTRICT JUDGE AT [PLACE]`
- **First instance (where District Judge has delegated jurisdiction under Section 33(1)):** `IN THE COURT OF THE CIVIL JUDGE (SR. DN.) AT [PLACE]`
- **Appellate:** Appellate Bench of the Court of Small Causes, Mumbai (for Mumbai) / District Judge (other districts) — Section 34
- **Revisional:** High Court of Bombay — Section 35 MRC Act 1999 + Article 227 of the Constitution

## Eviction grounds (section-by-section)

| Section | Ground | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| 16(1)(a) | Non-payment of standard rent and permitted increases | Read with Section 15 (rent default + neglect to pay within 90 days of demand notice) |
| 16(1)(b) | Unlawful sub-letting / assignment / transfer | Without written consent of landlord |
| 16(1)(c) | Nuisance or annoyance | By tenant or person residing with tenant |
| 16(1)(d) | Damage to premises | Act of waste / damage by tenant |
| 16(1)(e) | Change of user | Use for purpose other than that for which let |
| 16(1)(f) | Acquisition of alternate accommodation | Tenant has acquired vacant possession of suitable residence |
| 16(1)(g) | Bona-fide and reasonable requirement | Landlord requires the premises bona fide for own occupation / family / business |
| 16(1)(h) | Premises required for repairs / rebuilding | Substantial repairs not otherwise feasible |
| 16(1)(i) | Premises required for demolition + reconstruction | Subject to right of re-entry under Section 17 |
| 16(1)(j) | Erection of permanent structure | Without landlord's written consent |
| 16(1)(k) | Use contrary to terms | Breach of tenancy condition |
| 16(1)(l) | Premises required by trust / corporate landlord | For its bona-fide purpose |
| 16(1)(m)-(p) | Other statutory grounds | Including non-occupation for 6 months, etc. |

## Section 106 TPA notice position
- **REQUIRED.** Maharashtra Rent Control Act 1999 does NOT displace the requirement of notice under Section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act 1882.
- The position in **V. Dhanapal Chettiar v. Yesodai Ammal, (1979) 4 SCC 214** (which holds Section 106 notice unnecessary where the State Rent Act provides a self-contained eviction code) is held inapplicable to MRC Act 1999 by a consistent line of Bombay High Court authority.
- Practical discipline: serve Section 106 TPA notice along with Section 15 default-of-rent demand notice.

## Standard rent / fair rent fixation methodology
- **Section 7** — "Standard rent" defined; either (a) rent fixed by the Court, or (b) rent at which premises were let on 1 October 1987, or (c) cost-of-construction + return formula
- **Section 8** — Application for fixation of standard rent
- **Section 10** — Permitted increases for amenities, structural repairs, taxes
- **Section 11** — 4% annual increase in rent permitted

## Rent deposit procedure
- **Section 8(4) / Section 24** — Rent deposit with Court of Small Causes (Mumbai) / Civil Court (other districts)
- Tenant may deposit rent in Court where landlord refuses to accept; deposit operates as valid tender

## Summary procedure (if any)
- NO formal summary procedure equivalent to Section 25B Delhi Rent Control Act 1958
- Ordinary suit procedure applies; however Section 33 prescribes the exclusive forum

## Pagdi / customary tenancy (if any)
- **Section 56** — PAGDI SYSTEM STATUTORILY RECOGNIZED (Maharashtra was the first State to give statutory recognition to the pagdi customary tenancy framework)
- Transfer of pagdi tenancy permitted with landlord's consent; landlord entitled to share in pagdi consideration
- **Section 7(15)** — Heir-succession framework for pagdi and contractual tenancies

## Model Tenancy Act 2021 adoption status
- **NOT YET ADOPTED** in Maharashtra
- Proposal under consideration; State Government has not notified adoption as of current verification

## State-specific notes
- **Section 33** prescribes EXCLUSIVE forum — wrong forum draws Section 33 bar
- **Section 34** governs appeals; **Section 35** governs revisional jurisdiction of the High Court
- **Section 11(4)** interim deposit application is standard practice — tenant disputing standard rent must deposit admitted rent pending fixation
- **Section 17** preserves tenant's right of re-entry where eviction was on demolition/reconstruction ground and reconstruction is completed
- Mumbai's Appellate Bench of Court of Small Causes is a specialised appellate forum unique to Maharashtra rent jurisprudence
- Premises let to certain categories (banks, public-sector undertakings, foreign missions, multinational companies, etc.) may be exempt under Section 3 — verify exemption schedule before filing
