# State Exemplar — Karnataka (Rent Control)

## Applicable State Rent Control Act
- **Act:** Karnataka Rent Act 2001 (Karnataka Act No. 34 of 2001)
- **Scope:** Premises in notified urban areas of Karnataka let for residence, business or any other purpose
- **Replaces:** Karnataka Rent Control Act 1961

## Forum nomenclature
- **First instance (Bengaluru, premises monthly rent above ₹3,500 or otherwise within pecuniary jurisdiction):** `IN THE COURT OF SMALL CAUSES AT BENGALURU`
- **First instance (other places / lower-rent premises in Bengaluru):** `IN THE COURT OF THE CIVIL JUDGE (SR. DN.) AT [PLACE] AS RENT CONTROLLER`
- **Appellate:** District Judge — Section 28 of Karnataka Rent Act 2001
- **Revisional:** High Court of Karnataka — Article 227 of the Constitution + Section 115 CPC

## Eviction grounds (section-by-section)

| Section | Ground | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| 27(2)(a) | Non-payment of rent | Default for 2 months continuously after notice of demand |
| 27(2)(b) | Unlawful sub-letting / assignment / parting with possession | Without landlord's written consent |
| 27(2)(c) | Misuser / change of user | Use for purpose other than that for which let |
| 27(2)(d) | Acts of waste / damage | By tenant impairing value or utility of premises |
| 27(2)(e) | Nuisance / annoyance | By tenant or person residing with tenant |
| 27(2)(f) | Acquisition of alternate accommodation | Tenant has acquired vacant possession of suitable residence |
| 27(2)(g) | Non-occupation | Premises not occupied without reasonable cause for 4 months immediately preceding suit |
| 27(2)(h) | Erection of permanent structure | Without landlord's written consent |
| 27(2)(i) | Illegal / immoral purpose | Premises used for illegal / immoral purpose |
| 27(2)(j)-(q) | Other statutory grounds | Including breach of tenancy condition, change of user, etc. |
| 27(2)(r) | Bona-fide and reasonable requirement of landlord | For own occupation / family / business |
| 27(2)(s) | Premises required for repairs / rebuilding | Substantial repairs / demolition + reconstruction |

## Section 106 TPA notice position
- **SUBSTANTIALLY DISPLACED.** Section 31 of Karnataka Rent Act 2001 prescribes its own notice procedure for eviction proceedings.
- The position in **V. Dhanapal Chettiar v. Yesodai Ammal, (1979) 4 SCC 214** applies — the State Act provides a self-contained code; Section 106 TPA notice is not separately required for grounds under Section 27(2).
- Best practice: serve a composite notice referencing both Section 31 KRA 2001 and Section 106 TPA to forestall any technical objection.

## Standard rent / fair rent fixation methodology
- **Section 14** — Fair rent fixation by Rent Controller
- Formula: cost of construction + market value of site + reasonable return on capital + statutory permitted increases
- **Section 15-17** — Permitted increases for repairs, taxes, amenities

## Rent deposit procedure
- **Section 21** — Tenant may deposit rent with Rent Controller / Court of Small Causes where landlord refuses to accept
- Deposit operates as valid tender; defeats default-of-rent eviction ground

## Summary procedure (if any)
- **Sections 23-26** — Special / accelerated procedure for certain categories (premises required for landlord's bona-fide occupation, etc.)
- NO Chapter IIIA DRC-equivalent strict summary track; however the Act provides time-bound disposal directions

## Pagdi / customary tenancy (if any)
- NOT statutorily recognized in Karnataka

## Model Tenancy Act 2021 adoption status
- **NOT ADOPTED.** Karnataka Rent Act 2001 continues
- MTA 2021 adoption under State Government consideration; no notification as of current verification

## State-specific notes
- **₹3,500 monthly rent threshold** — Section 2(3) of Karnataka Rent Act 2001 excludes premises with monthly rent exceeding ₹3,500 from the protective provisions of certain sections (notably standard-rent fixation and some eviction protections); such premises are governed by contractual / TPA framework with ordinary civil court jurisdiction
- **Court of Small Causes Bengaluru** has special jurisdiction over Bengaluru rent matters within its pecuniary limits; outside Bengaluru, the Civil Judge (Sr. Dn.) functions as Rent Controller
- **Section 27(4)** — bona-fide requirement ground subject to comparative hardship test
- **Section 30** — restriction on filing fresh eviction proceedings on same ground after dismissal
- **Section 32-33** — execution of eviction orders; Rent Controller has powers of a Civil Court for execution
- Pre-2001 tenancies that arose under the 1961 Act are now governed by transitional provisions of the 2001 Act — verify applicable framework on date of tenancy commencement
