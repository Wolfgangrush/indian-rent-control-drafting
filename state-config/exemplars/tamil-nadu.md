# State Exemplar — Tamil Nadu (Rent Control)

## Applicable State Rent Control Act
- **Current Act (post-22-Feb-2019 tenancies):** Tamil Nadu Regulation of Rights and Responsibilities of Landlords and Tenants Act 2017 (Tamil Nadu Act No. 42 of 2017) — brought into force 22 February 2019
- **Historical Act (pre-22-Feb-2019 tenancies / continuing protection):** Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act 1960
- **Scope (2017 Act):** Compulsory written tenancy agreement + registration with Rent Authority within 90 days; covers residential and non-residential premises in the State (with notified exemptions)

## Forum nomenclature
- **First instance (2017 Act, registration / standard rent / deposit / ordinary disputes):** `BEFORE THE RENT AUTHORITY, [PLACE]`
- **First instance (2017 Act, eviction cases above prescribed pecuniary value / appeals from Rent Authority):** `BEFORE THE RENT TRIBUNAL, [PLACE]`
- **First instance (1960 Act, for pre-2019 tenancies):** `BEFORE THE RENT CONTROLLER, [PLACE]` (Principal District Munsif or designated Court)
- **Appellate (1960 Act):** Appellate Authority (Sub-Court / District Court)
- **Appellate (2017 Act):** Rent Tribunal (from Rent Authority orders)
- **Revisional:** High Court of Madras — Article 227 of the Constitution; Section 25 of 1960 Act provides revisional jurisdiction for tenancies governed by 1960 Act

## Section 106 TPA notice position
- **NOT MANDATORY.** Both the 1960 Act and the 2017 Act are self-contained codes for eviction.
- Position in **V. Dhanapal Chettiar v. Yesodai Ammal, (1979) 4 SCC 214** directly applies — and indeed the case arose under the Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act 1960.

## Standard rent / fair rent fixation methodology
- **1960 Act, Section 4** — Fair rent fixation by Rent Controller; cost-of-construction + market value of site + reasonable return formula
- **2017 Act** — Rent as agreed in registered tenancy agreement; Rent Authority adjudicates disputes regarding standard rent

## Rent deposit procedure
- **1960 Act, Section 8** — Tenant may deposit rent with Rent Controller upon landlord's refusal to accept
- **2017 Act** — Rent deposit with Rent Authority

## Summary procedure (if any)
- **2017 Act** — Rent Authority adjudicates within 60 days of application; Rent Tribunal disposes appeals within 120 days (time-bound, designed for speed)
- NO Chapter IIIA-equivalent summary procedure under 1960 Act

## Pagdi / customary tenancy (if any)
- NOT statutorily recognized in Tamil Nadu

## Model Tenancy Act 2021 adoption status
- **MODIFIED ADOPTER.** Tamil Nadu's 2017 Act largely PREDATED and now PARALLELS the MTA 2021 framework — compulsory written agreement, registration, security deposit caps, Rent Authority + Rent Tribunal structure
- Security deposit cap (Section 5 of 2017 Act): **2 months** rent (residential) / **6 months** rent (non-residential) — broadly aligned with MTA 2021 caps
- Tamil Nadu has not formally "adopted" MTA 2021 because the 2017 Act already substantially covers the same ground

## State-specific notes
- **DUAL FORUM STRUCTURE** — Old tenancies (commenced before 22 February 2019) remain under 1960 Act → Rent Controller forum. New tenancies (commenced on or after 22 February 2019) fall under 2017 Act → Rent Authority forum. Always verify date of tenancy commencement before choosing forum.
- **Compulsory written agreement** under Section 4 of 2017 Act — oral tenancies entered after 22 February 2019 are unprotected
- **90-day registration window** under 2017 Act — failure to register attracts presumptions adverse to defaulting party
- **Time-bound disposal** under 2017 Act (60 days at Rent Authority / 120 days at Tribunal) is statutorily mandated — distinct from open-ended 1960 Act proceedings
- Madras High Court has held that the 2017 Act does not apply retrospectively to tenancies created before 22 February 2019 (verify current authority)
