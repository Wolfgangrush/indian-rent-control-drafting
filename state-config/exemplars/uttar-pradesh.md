# State Exemplar — Uttar Pradesh (Rent Control)

## Applicable State Rent Control Act
- **Act:** Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act 1972 (U.P. Act No. 13 of 1972)
- **Scope:** Buildings in urban areas of Uttar Pradesh let for residence, business or any other purpose
- **Key exemption:** Buildings constructed within the last 10 years are exempt from rent-control protective provisions (Section 2(2)) — only TPA / contractual framework applies

## Forum nomenclature
- **First instance (Section 21 release applications — bona-fide need):** `BEFORE THE PRESCRIBED AUTHORITY, [PLACE]` (a Civil Judge designated as Prescribed Authority — administrative-judicial hybrid forum unique to UP)
- **First instance (ordinary eviction suits):** `IN THE COURT OF JUDGE SMALL CAUSES, [PLACE]`
- **First instance (where premises rent-control exempt):** `IN THE COURT OF THE CIVIL JUDGE / JUDGE SMALL CAUSES, [PLACE]` (under TPA / CPC)
- **Appellate:** District Judge — Section 22 of the 1972 Act (appeals from Prescribed Authority orders) / Civil Judge (appeals in ordinary suits)
- **Revisional:** High Court of Allahabad — Article 227 of the Constitution + Section 25 of the 1972 Act for some categories + Section 115 CPC

## Eviction grounds (section-by-section)

| Section | Ground | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| 20(2)(a) | Non-payment of rent | Default for 4 months despite notice of demand |
| 20(2)(b) | Sub-letting | Without landlord's written consent |
| 20(2)(c) | Misuser | Use for purpose other than that for which let |
| 20(2)(d) | Acts of waste / damage | By tenant |
| 20(2)(e) | Nuisance / annoyance | By tenant or person residing with tenant |
| 20(2)(f) | Acquisition of alternate accommodation | Tenant has acquired suitable alternate residential / commercial accommodation |
| 20(2)(g) | Structural alteration | Without landlord's written consent |
| 20(4) | Tenant deposit defence | One-time defence: tenant may deposit entire arrears + interest + costs on first hearing → suit dismissed (one-time-only) |
| 21(1)(a) | Bona-fide need of landlord | RELEASE APPLICATION before Prescribed Authority — landlord's bona-fide need for residential / commercial premises for self / family |
| 21(1)(b) | Premises required for demolition / reconstruction | Subject to safeguards |
| 21(8) | Comparative hardship test | Mandatory consideration in Section 21 release applications |

## Section 106 TPA notice position
- **SUBSTANTIALLY DISPLACED** for Section 21 release applications (which proceed before Prescribed Authority under self-contained UP statutory procedure).
- **REQUIRED** for ordinary eviction suits under Section 20 in the Court of Judge Small Causes (composite notice under Section 20 + Section 106 TPA is best practice).
- For rent-control-exempt premises (Section 2(2) — buildings less than 10 years old): full Section 106 TPA notice mandatory.

## Standard rent / fair rent fixation methodology
- **Section 5** — Standard rent provisions
- **Section 9** — Determination of reasonable annual rent by District Magistrate / Prescribed Authority
- Formula: ad-valorem on building cost + reasonable return + permitted statutory adjustments
- **Section 8** — Allotment / release of buildings by District Magistrate (regulatory function distinct from rent fixation)

## Rent deposit procedure
- **Section 30** — Tenant may deposit rent in Court (Munsif / Judge Small Causes) upon landlord's refusal to accept
- **Section 20(4)** — One-time deposit defence: deposit of arrears + 9% interest + costs on first hearing of eviction suit defeats Section 20(2)(a) default ground (available once in tenant's lifetime as tenant of that premises)

## Summary procedure (if any)
- Section 21 release application before Prescribed Authority is structured to be relatively expeditious — affidavit-based evidence permitted
- NO Chapter IIIA DRC-equivalent strict leave-to-defend procedure
- Section 34(1) — Prescribed Authority and Appellate Authority have certain powers of a Civil Court

## Pagdi / customary tenancy (if any)
- NOT statutorily recognized in Uttar Pradesh

## Model Tenancy Act 2021 adoption status
- **IN PROCESS.** UP draft Bill 2021 / 2022 (Uttar Pradesh Urban Premises Tenancy Regulation Bill) prepared and circulated; **awaiting notification** — verify current status with State Government's Housing Department before drafting
- If notified, will likely repeal / supersede the 1972 Act for new tenancies (transitional mechanism expected)

## State-specific notes
- **DUAL FORUM STRUCTURE** — Section 21 release applications → Prescribed Authority. Section 20 ordinary eviction suits → Judge Small Causes. Choosing the wrong forum is fatal; the two procedures are structurally distinct.
- **Prescribed Authority forum** is UP's distinctive contribution to Indian rent-control jurisprudence — administrative-judicial hybrid that adjudicates bona-fide need with comparative hardship test (Section 21(8)) and mandatory time-bound disposal targets
- **10-year exemption rule** (Section 2(2)) — buildings constructed within 10 years preceding the operative date are exempt from rent-control protective provisions; only TPA / contractual framework applies. Always verify date of construction before invoking 1972 Act protections
- **Section 20(4) one-time deposit defence** — strategic instrument; tenant exhausting this defence once cannot rely on it again
- **Section 25** — restricted revisional remedy from Prescribed Authority orders to High Court
- **District Magistrate's allotment / release powers under Section 8 / Section 16** — regulatory layer overlaid on the tenancy framework; allotment proceedings can interact with eviction proceedings
- Pre-1972 tenancies governed by the Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act 1947 transitioned under saving provisions
