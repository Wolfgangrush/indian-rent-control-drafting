# State Exemplar — West Bengal (Rent Control)

## Applicable State Rent Control Act
- **Act:** West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act 1997 (West Bengal Act No. 37 of 1997)
- **Scope:** Premises in West Bengal let for residence, business or any other purpose; supersedes the West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act 1956 for tenancies post-commencement
- **Replaces:** West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act 1956 (transitional provisions for continuing tenancies)

## Forum nomenclature
- **First instance (Kolkata):** `BEFORE THE CONTROLLER OF RENT AT KOLKATA`
- **First instance (mofussil / other districts):** `BEFORE THE CONTROLLER, [PLACE]`
- **Appellate:** Appellate Authority constituted under Section 30 of the 1997 Act (District Judge or Additional District Judge)
- **Revisional:** High Court of Calcutta — Article 227 of the Constitution

## Eviction grounds (section-by-section)

| Section | Ground | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| 6(1)(a) | Non-payment of rent | Default in payment of rent for any month within prescribed time |
| 6(1)(b) | Nuisance / annoyance | By tenant or person residing with tenant |
| 6(1)(c) | Damage to premises | Acts of waste / impairment |
| 6(1)(d) | Misuser | Use for purpose other than that for which premises let |
| 6(1)(e) | Sub-letting / parting with possession | Without landlord's written consent |
| 6(1)(f) | Bona-fide requirement of landlord | For own occupation / family / business |
| 6(1)(g) | Acquisition of alternate accommodation | Tenant has acquired suitable alternate accommodation |
| 6(1)(h) | Premises required for repairs / rebuilding | Substantial repairs not feasible while in occupation |
| 6(1)(i) | Illegal / immoral purpose | Use for illegal or immoral purpose |
| 6(1)(j) | Structural alteration | Without landlord's written consent |
| 6(1)(k) | Non-occupation | Premises not occupied without reasonable cause |
| 6(1)(l)-(p) | Other statutory grounds | Including change of user, breach of tenancy condition, premises required by trust/corporate landlord, etc. |
| 6(1)(o) | Illegal user | Continued illegal user despite notice |

## Section 106 TPA notice position
- **REQUIRED.** The West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act 1997 does NOT displace the requirement of notice under Section 106 of the Transfer of Property Act 1882.
- Calcutta High Court jurisprudence consistently holds that for grounds requiring determination of contractual tenancy, Section 106 TPA notice precedes invocation of Section 6 grounds.
- Practical discipline: serve composite notice under Section 6 (specifying ground) read with Section 106 TPA.

## Standard rent / fair rent fixation methodology
- **Sections 17-22** — Standard rent / fair rent provisions
- **Section 17** — Determination of fair rent by Controller
- Formula: market rent + cost-of-construction + permitted statutory increases
- **Section 19-22** — Permitted increases for repairs, taxes, amenities, structural improvements

## Rent deposit procedure
- **Section 21** — Tenant may deposit rent with Controller upon landlord's refusal to accept
- Deposit must be made within prescribed time to be effective as valid tender
- Defeats Section 6(1)(a) default ground if statutory conditions complied with

## Summary procedure (if any)
- NO Chapter IIIA DRC-equivalent strict summary procedure
- Ordinary procedure before Controller applies; Controller has wide procedural discretion under Section 27

## Pagdi / customary tenancy (if any)
- NOT statutorily recognized in West Bengal
- Customary "salami" / premium payments in Kolkata exist but draw no specific statutory protection under the 1997 Act

## Model Tenancy Act 2021 adoption status
- **NOT ADOPTED.** West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act 1997 continues
- No MTA 2021 adoption notification by State Government as of current verification

## State-specific notes
- **Transitional discipline** — Tenancies that arose under the 1956 Act are now governed by the 1997 Act with transitional provisions under Section 47; certain rights crystallised under the 1956 Act are preserved
- **Section 6(4)** — bona-fide requirement ground subject to comparative hardship test; Controller must weigh hardship to tenant against hardship to landlord
- **Section 7** — Restriction on eviction of certain categories of tenants (long-term tenants of 20+ years' standing in certain categories may have additional protection)
- **Section 27** — Controller has wide powers to summon witnesses, examine on oath, regulate procedure
- **Section 35** — Bar of jurisdiction of Civil Courts in respect of matters which the Controller is empowered to decide
- **Kolkata-specific:** Controller of Rent at Kolkata exercises jurisdiction over premises within the Kolkata Municipal Corporation limits; mofussil Controllers exercise district-level jurisdiction
- Land and Land Reforms Department of the State Government is the administrative authority overseeing Controllers
